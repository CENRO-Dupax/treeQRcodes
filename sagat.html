SAGAT

(Mesua ferrea)

The Sagat tree (Mesua ferrea), also known as Ironwood, is a medium to large evergreen tree that can reach up to 30 meters in height. It has a straight trunk with a diameter of up to 2 meters, often featuring a deeply fissured, dark brown bark. The leaves are glossy, dark green, lance-shaped, and leathery, with new leaves emerging in shades of pink to red. Flowers are large, fragrant, and white, with four petals and numerous yellow stamens. The fruit is a capsule that splits open when mature, releasing seeds. Sagat is known for its dense, hard wood, making it highly durable and resistant to decay.

COMMON USES

Sagat is a valuable tree with a variety of uses. Its dense, durable wood is highly prized for heavy construction, including furniture, beams, and railway sleepers. In traditional medicine, various parts of the tree—such as its flowers, seeds, bark, and leaves—are used to treat ailments like fever, skin diseases, asthma, cough, and rheumatism. The tree is also favored for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks due to its attractive foliage and fragrant flowers. Additionally, the seeds yield medicinal oil for skincare and rheumatism, while the flowers are used in perfumery and religious offerings.
