BENGUET PINE

(Pinus insularis)

Benguet pine is a tall tree, growing 30 to 40 meters with a 140 cm diameter, characterized by dark brown, deeply fissured bark, numerous resin canals, spreading branches, a narrow crown with weak lateral branches, dark green needles in fascicles of two or three up to 22 cm long, and ovoid brown cones 3-5 cm in diameter, occurring singly or in pairs.

COMMON USES

Baguio pine has limited folkloric medicinal uses in the Philippines, with latex traditionally rubbed on arthritic pains in the Mountain Province, and historically, its oil was used to treat lung infections and revitalize individuals suffering from mental or emotional fatigue when added to baths. It is also a source of Philippine turpentine oil, commercially used during Spanish times. The soft and light timber of Baguio pine is versatile, finding applications in the production of boxes, roundwood poles, construction timber, plywood, furniture, and floorboards, as well as being used as pulpwood in the paper industry. Additionally, Native Americans historically stuffed mattresses with pine needles to repel lice and fleas.
