AKLENG PARANG

(Albizia procera)

Albizia procera is a deciduous tree with an open canopy that typically grows between 7 and 15 meters tall, occasionally reaching up to 30 meters. It has a trunk diameter of 35-60 cm, a straight or crooked bole up to 9 meters, and smooth bark that is pale grey-green, yellowish, or brownish, sometimes flaking to reveal an orange color beneath. The tree's bipinnate leaves have 2-5 pairs of sub-opposite pinnae and a rachis of 10-30 cm, while its greenish-yellow flowers with long, threadlike white stamens form puffball-like clusters on racemes up to 25 cm long. The flowers give way to dark red or reddish-brown flattened pods, 11.5-20 cm long, containing 6-12 small seeds. The tree is leafless during the dry season (August-September) and features both epigeal seed germination and glabrous branchlets.

COMMON USES

Albizia procera has diverse uses across various applications. Its leaves and pods are edible, and in times of scarcity, its bark can be ground into powder, mixed with flour, and consumed. While no folkloric medicinal uses are reported in the Philippines, in India, the leaves are poulticed for ulcers and blisters, the bark is used for pregnancy and stomachache, and its decoction is employed for rheumatism and hemorrhage. The bark is also considered spermicidal, and powdered seeds are used to treat amoebiasis, urinary tract infections, hemorrhoids, and worms. In Thailand, it is included in herbal recipes for pain relief. Beyond medicinal uses, the tree is valuable in agroforestry for amenity planting, wind-breaks, and soil rehabilitation. It provides fuelwood with high calorific value, and its wood is sought after for crafting, making furniture, cabinetry, and paper pulp. Additionally, in India and Nepal, the leaves are used as fodder, the bark is used as fish poison, and the leaves serve as an insecticide
