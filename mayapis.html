MAYAPIS

(Dipterocarpus turbinatus)

Mayapis is a tall, majestic tree, reaching heights of 30 to 45 meters, with a cylindrical trunk up to 2.5 meters in diameter. Its bark is thick, gray or dark brown, and flaky with shallow fissures. The tree's branchlets are often marked with lenticels and can be glabrous or pubescent. The leaves are alternate, spiral, ovate to elliptic, and glossy above, with petioles up to 5 cm long. The tree produces 3-7 white or yellow flowers with a pink tinge, arranged in axillary spikes. The fruits are globular to ellipsoid, with prominent striations, and feature long, linear wings that can grow up to 17.5 cm. The tree's flowers and fruits contribute to its reproductive process and are a distinctive feature.

COMMON USES

Dipterocarpus turbinatus is valued in traditional medicine and industry. Its resin is used to treat ulcers, ringworm, and gonorrhea, and is applied topically for eczema, cuts, and wounds. In Chinese medicine, borneol oil from the tree alleviates itching and pain, while in India, oleoresin treats conditions like tuberculosis, leprosy, psoriasis, and indolent ulcers. The tree's bark decoction is used for rheumatism, and its oil acts as a stimulant to the genitourinary tract and a diuretic. The tree provides keruing wood, used in plywood and as fuel, and is a significant source of borneol, a valuable aromatic essential oil.
